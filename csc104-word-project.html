MS Word Assignment: Business Proposal

Follow the instructions below to create the proposal in Microsoft Word.

Your Scenario

Imagine you are a Project Manager at Innovate Solutions. You've had a promising initial meeting with a potential client, "The Morning Grind," and now you need to create a professional proposal to win their business. While your team is still finalizing some of the specific marketing copy, your task is to build the document's structure and format the content you already have.

Please use only the text provided in the instructions below. Do not add or generate your own content to fill out the proposal.

Part 1: Overall Document & Page Setup

Begin by opening a new blank document in Word and applying the following settings to the entire file.

  • Theme: Apply the "Office" theme.
  • Margins: Set the page margins to "Narrow".
  • Watermark: Add a "Confidential" watermark.
  • Page Border: Apply a simple "Box" page border.
  • Document Properties: In the document's info/properties, set the "Subject" field to `Marketing Proposal`.

Part 2: Cover Page, Header & Footer

Create the framing elements for your professional document.

  • Cover Page: Insert the built-in "Facet" style cover page and fill in the following details:
    • Document Title: Website & Social Media Proposal
    • Document Subtitle: Prepared for The Morning Grind
    • Author: Your Name
    • Company: Innovate Solutions
  • Header: Insert a header that is different on the first page. Starting on page 2, the header must contain the text "Proposal for The Morning Grind", aligned to the right.
  • Footer: Insert a footer that contains a centered page number on all pages except the cover page.

Part 3: Proposal Letter

On the second page of your document, create the proposal letter using the content below and apply the specified formatting.

  • Letterhead: Create a simple letterhead by inserting a Rectangle Shape at the top of the page. Apply the "Subtle Effect - Blue, Accent 1" shape style and add the text "Innovate Solutions" to the shape. Format this text to be 16pt and Bold.
  • Bold Formatting: Use the Find and Replace tool to find all instances of "The Morning Grind" in the letter and make them Bold.
  • Numbered List: Format the three project phases (starting with "Phase 1...") as a numbered list.
  • Paragraph Shading: Apply a "Blue, Accent 1, Lighter 80%" background shading to the paragraph that begins "Our team is confident...".
  • Picture: Insert an online picture of a coffee cup. Resize it to be 2 inches wide, apply the "Metal Oval" picture style, and set the text wrapping to "Square". Position it to the right of the numbered list.
  • Footnote: At the end of the sentence "...drive customer engagement.", insert a footnote with the text: `Source: 2023 Digital Marketing Report.`

Content for Proposal Letter

October 26, 2025

Mr. David Chen
Owner, The Morning Grind
123 Main Street
Anytown, USA 12345

Dear Mr. Chen,

Thank you for considering Innovate Solutions for your digital marketing needs. We are excited about the opportunity to partner with The Morning Grind to enhance your online presence. We specialize in modern, responsive web design and targeted social media strategies that drive customer engagement.

Our proposed project is divided into three clear phases:

Phase 1: Website Development. This includes a full site design, mobile optimization, and an online menu page.
Phase 2: Social Media Launch. We will create and manage official profiles on three major platforms.
Phase 3: Ongoing Content & Analytics. We will provide weekly content updates and a monthly performance report.

Our team is confident that this proposal for The Morning Grind outlines a path to significant growth. We look forward to the possibility of working together.

Sincerely,

[Your Name]
Project Manager
Innovate Solutions

Part 4: Proposal Content Sections

On a new page after the letter (insert a Page Break), add the following content sections. Apply the specified styles and formatting.

  • Headings: For the titles "Project Goals & Approach" and "Detailed Scope of Work", apply the Heading 1 style.
  • Bulleted List: Under "Detailed Scope of Work", format the list of website features (starting with "Custom 5-page design...") as a standard bulleted list.

Content for Sections

Project Goals & Approach

Our primary goal is to increase online visibility and drive foot traffic to The Morning Grind. We will achieve this by creating a modern, user-friendly website that showcases your unique brand and products. Our social media strategy will focus on building a community of loyal customers through engaging content and targeted promotions.

Detailed Scope of Work

The project will include the following deliverables:

Custom 5-page design (Home, About, Menu, Blog, Contact)
Mobile-responsive layout for all devices
Online menu with high-quality images
Setup of three social media profiles (e.g., Instagram, Facebook, Twitter)
Initial content creation and a one-month content calendar

Part 5: Costs & Timeline

On a new page after the content sections, add the project's cost breakdown and timeline.

  • Title: Type "Project Cost Breakdown" and apply the Heading 1 style.
  • Table: Insert a table using the data provided below.
    • Apply the "Grid Table 5 Dark - Accent 1" table style.
    • Center all content within the table cells both horizontally and vertically.
    • In the final row, merge the cells and enter the text "Total Cost: $2,100", formatted in Bold.
  • SmartArt: Below the table, insert a "Basic Process" SmartArt graphic.
    • Enter the three project phases into the shapes: `Website Development`, `Social Media Launch`, and `Content & Analytics`.
    • Change the colors to "Colorful Range - Accent Colors 1 to 2".
    • Apply the "Intense Effect" SmartArt style.

Data for Cost Breakdown Table
